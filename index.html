About Yael

Your Guide Through Every Step of Buying Property in Israel

Buying property in Israel can feel overwhelming — a different language, unfamiliar legal systems, complex mortgage processes, and a fast-moving market. That's where I come in.

I've personally guided clients through the purchase of over 30 apartments across Israel. But I'm not just here to show you listings. I help you figure out what you actually need, navigate the bureaucracy, connect you with trusted lawyers and mortgage brokers, and stay by your side from first conversation to renovation completion.

Whether you're buying your first home, investing from abroad, or relocating to Israel — I make the process clear, safe, and even enjoyable.

You don't need to be wealthy to own property in Israel. With down payments starting from around ₪200,000 (~$70K USD) depending on conditions, owning an apartment is more accessible than most people think. We use creative financial planning — such as structuring rent to cover your mortgage — to help clients afford properties they thought were beyond reach.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Buying Property in Israel

Yes, foreigners can buy property in Israel with no restrictions on private property ownership. International buyers need an Israeli lawyer, a local bank account, and proper documentation. Israel Property Advisors guides foreign buyers through every step — from property search to keys in hand.

Property prices vary by location. Jerusalem and Tel Aviv are the most expensive markets, while cities like Beit Shemesh, Netanya, and Haifa offer more affordable options. Down payments can start from approximately $70,000 USD (₪200,000), depending on the property and mortgage terms.

Yes, foreign buyers can obtain mortgages from Israeli banks, typically financing up to 50% of the property value. We connect clients with experienced mortgage brokers who specialize in foreign buyer financing and use creative financial strategies — like structuring rental income to cover mortgage payments.

Foreign buyers pay purchase tax (mas rechisha) ranging from approximately 8–10% of the property value. Israeli citizens and new immigrants (olim) benefit from reduced tax rates. Additional costs include lawyer fees, mortgage fees, and registration costs. We help clients understand the full cost breakdown before purchasing.

Popular cities for property investment include Jerusalem, Tel Aviv, Beit Shemesh, Netanya, Haifa, and Be'er Sheva. Each market has different price points, rental yields, and growth potential. We help investors evaluate opportunities based on their budget and investment goals.
